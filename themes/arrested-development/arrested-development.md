# Arrested Development Theme

> "I don't understand the question, and I won't respond to it."

A collection of spinner verbs inspired by the Bluth family's dysfunction, featuring callbacks from all seasons of the series. From banana stand economics to never nude fashion, these verbs capture the spirit of one of television's most quotable comedies.

## Verb Reference

| Verb | Reference |
|------|-----------|
| Making a Huge Mistake | "I've made a huge mistake" - Gob's catchphrase |
| Blue-ing Myself | Tobias accidentally joining Blue Man Group/his paint incidents |
| Leaving Notes | "There's always money in the banana stand" - George Sr.'s hidden cash |
| Doing Chicken Impressions | The Bluth family's hilariously wrong chicken dances |
| Never Nude-ing | Tobias's condition requiring jean cutoffs at all times |
| Burning Down the Banana Stand | Michael's literal interpretation of his father's advice |
| Saying Hey Brother | Buster's warm greeting to Michael |
| Waiting for the Stair Car | The Bluth Company's impractical company vehicle |
| Doing Illusions Not Tricks | "Illusions, Michael. A trick is something a whore does for money." |

| Hopping Off the Balcony | "There's always money in the..." followed by dramatic falls |
| Calling Barry | Barry Zuckerkorn, the incompetent family lawyer |
| Hiding in the Attic | George Sr.'s time hiding from the law |

| Motherboying | Buster and Lucille's uncomfortable closeness |
| Attending Army | Buster's brief military career |
| Popping a Forget-Me-Now | Gob's roofie-based solution to awkward situations |

| Avoiding the Secular Tendencies | Maeby's fake identity at school |

| Listening for the Beads | Ann's barely noticeable presence - "Her?" |
| Preparing an Object Lesson | J. Walter Weatherman's elaborate life lessons |
| Seeking Anustart | Tobias's unfortunate license plate (A New Start) |
